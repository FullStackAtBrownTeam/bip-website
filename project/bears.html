BEARS
Overall Purpose
The BEARS team of BIP is advocating for new legislation in Rhode Island that will ensure that teachers across the state are trained in a series of “Basic Emergency and Response Skills” (BEARS). These skills include CPR training, bleeding control, and choking protocol. We also may add infectious disease prevention training to the mix this year, as COVID-19 has necessitated this skill among school faculty. In the past, we completed a comprehensive literature review of the effect such training would have on the health and safety of Rhode Island’s students. Finally, we met with teachers’ unions, nurses’ unions, and other public health groups throughout the state of Rhode Island to obtain their feedback on BEARS training.
Goals of the Project for the Fall Semester
This semester, we will be finalizing the BEARS protocol and testing it through our pilot program; a series of virtual training sessions with Rhode Island schools. On the advocacy end of things, we will be working as much as we can with stakeholders to make sure that the BEARS bill is the best that it can be. We will be working with union reps, teachers, students, school nurses, principals, emergency medical professionals and many other interested members of the community here in Rhode Island. We will also be working with various lawmakers to help get the BEARS bill passed in next year’s legislative session. By the end of the semester, we will have tangible data on the efficacy of BEARS training and hopefully a prominent place in next year’s legislative agenda.
