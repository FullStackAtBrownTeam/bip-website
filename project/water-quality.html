Water Quality
Overall Purpose
The lead levels of Providence drinking water have exceeded the federal limit since 2007, a factor our team believes has contributed to lead poisoning cases in the area. Our goal is to address this public health issue by advocating for equitable full lead pipe replacement. The work we have done thus far has focused on developing a nuanced understanding of the issue — stakeholders, political levers, logistical barriers — as well as investigating existing lead pipe replacement models. All of this has culminated in a policy brief with legal and financial measures that, if implemented, could bring us closer to lead service line elimination in Providence.

More recently, we have been exploring how to include community voices into our work. Through engaging with Childhood Lead Action Project (CLAP), Providence Water Supply Board, and professors at Brown University School of Public Health, we have determined how we can do so most effectively: supplement existing lead contamination outreach. By working alongside Providence residents, we hope to encourage them to take advantage of existing resources and help this issue reach the government officials who hold the authority to bring about significant change.
Goals of the Project for the Fall Semester
This semester, our team will consolidate and distribute the research we have conducted on lead contamination and strategies to mitigate its harm. In order to ensure that this information is appropriately disseminated to communities most impacted by lead pipes, we will collect data on the geographical distribution of loan program participants and measure public awareness of lead contamination. In September, we will present our past work to the management of Providence Water, the largest water utility in Rhode Island. This is super exciting because we have the potential to help shape their approach to lead pipe replacement! We also anticipate this will refine our objectives for the coming year.
