Interested in joining us as a research analyst?
We welcome any Brown students interested in public policy with a passion for problem-solving and contributing meaningfully to the local community. BIP analysts get to collaborate with organizational partners, learn the ins-and-outs of policy research, and exercise their political chops advocating for sensible policy. No prior background in policy work is required. 
Interested in joining us in another capacity?
Every year, a critical contingent of BIP members work on internal projects (some with external- facing components) that ensure our club thrives and achieves our goals. This can include hosting events in the Brown community to raise awareness of policy issues, helping BIP apply for grant funding for our projects, and other engaging projects you will work on with a team of people.

Refugee Education Analyst
In June 2019, the Johns Hopkins for Education Policy published a report detailing the concerning and systemic failures of the Providence public school system. In light of the report’s findings, Providence Public Schools (PPSD) is currently undergoing a “state takeover.” Administration is being disseminated among state level leadership, in the hopes of stewarding the district away from ongoing decay.
BIP’s Refugee Education team, as its name implies, devotes attention towards a particular community we worry might be left out of the takeover process: the hundreds of refugee students currently living in the city. Using a comprehensive survey, we seek to assert how poor academic instruction, student fears about their safety, and marginalization of parents might especially harm these students and their families.
We are seeking to take on some more analysts for the upcoming semester. Desirable qualities for this position include: passion for community/civic engagement, excitement to work alongside a nonprofit, strong internet research/citation skills, and the capacity to read and engage with large quantities of academic literature.
BEARS Analyst
This semester new BEARS Analysts will be working to develop a health education curriculum, helping organize meetings and possibly trainings with RI schools, and learning the intricacies of a public health campaign in a solutions oriented stage from the inside. We are looking for someone with some healthcare related experience or coursework, and an excitement about BEARS as a whole!
Internal Affairs Analyst
This year, the new IA analyst will be working with an existing team to plan virtual events revolving around the topic of homelessness in Rhode Island, including how this population has been affected by the COVID-19 pandemic. They will also have the potential to be assisting with grant applications, facilitating a more active presence for BIP on social media, and brainstorming new strategic project management designs. If you are interested in completing tasks that require strategic-thinking, creativity, and great organizational skills, this is the right position for you!
